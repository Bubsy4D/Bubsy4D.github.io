The essential applications for a weekend trip in Grenoble

Mickael Pot, Matteo Clavel
24nd Oct, 2021
Grenoble, Weekend trip, Smartphone application
0 Comments

Any good holiday cannot be achieved without an excellent guide and our smartphone has become a great way to discover a destination for a weekend.
This week, let's focus on the capital of the Alps, Grenoble.
To help us quench our thirst for discovery, we got help from 3 applications. But not just any application. They had to be free, pleasant to use and compatible with iOs and Android systems.

Grenoble Alpes Métropole Pass

The first application is called Grenoble Alpes Métropole Pass.
This application offers you a list of activities that can be done in Grenoble, according to a choice among seven centers of interest (Museum, guided tour, leisure, cave, monument, transport, shop/grocery).
Unlike other applications, this one offers a pass, which can be purchased for a cost of 33 euros for a weekend.
You don't need the pass to access the activities proposed by the application but it will simplify your life by including the prices of those activities, public transportation, the rental of a bike for the weekend, a round trip ticket for the most emblematic activity of Grenoble, the cable car to go up to the Bastille, and discounts for complementary activities.
The application is functional but the purchase of the pass online returns an error. A bad point then. You can however buy the pass directly from the reception of your hotel or from the tourist office located in the heart of the city center. It’s this last choice that we have made.
We used the pass on a weekend and we wanted to use it as much as possible.
We were able to use it to access at the Grenoble museum, taste Grenoble's specialties, take a canoe trip on the Isère river, visit the Sassenages vats (which we reached with our provided bike) and do the Bastille’s rope adventure park. Concerning the acceptance of the pass by the activities places, they validated it without problem.
In the end, this saved us 40 euros. For that, a big validation for the pass.

Metromobilité

The second application is called Metromobilité.
It will allow you to join the different places mentioned. The application calculates your itinerary and suggests the best possible choice between tramway, bus, bike and .... walking.
The application is in real time and we haven’t seen any discrepancies between the advertised times and the actual conditions.

Komoot

The third application is called Komoot.
Integrating a GPS, the application suggests hikes around your location and accompanies you on the way. The hikes are suggested and rated by the users of the application.
We went for the Bastille’s hike and the application shows us the walking time, the difference in altitude and the distance to be covered. The application also displays our walking speed and calculates the distance and time remaining.

In conclusion, Grenoble is a city where you have to go off the beaten track to appreciate its beauty.
The three applications we tested allowed us to discover it and even to learn new things about it.
